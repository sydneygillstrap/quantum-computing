Enter the Void

What is Quantum Computing?

Classical computers use "Bits" (0 or 1). Imagine a coin on a table: it's either heads or tails. A Quantum Computer uses Qubits. Imagine the coin spinning on the table. While it's spinning, it is both heads and tails at the exact same time.

The Quantum Advantage

Where a classical computer searches a maze by trying every path one by one, a quantum computer explores every path simultaneously.

The Two Pillars

Superposition

The ability of a quantum system to be in multiple states at once. It only "chooses" a state when we observe it, collapsing the wave function.

Entanglement

Einstein called it "Spooky action at a distance." Two particles become linked; change the state of one, and the other changes instantly, even if it's on the other side of the galaxy.
